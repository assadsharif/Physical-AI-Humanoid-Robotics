# Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used throughout the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook. Terms are organized alphabetically.

---

## A

**Action Space**: The set of all possible actions a robot can take in a given state.

**Actuator**: A device that converts energy into motion, enabling robot movement.

---

## B

**Bipedal Locomotion**: Walking or running on two legs, characteristic of humanoid robots.

---

## C

**Compliant Control**: Control strategy that allows the robot to yield to external forces.

---

## D

**DCM (Divergent Component of Motion)**: A stability criterion for bipedal walking.

**DDS (Data Distribution Service)**: The middleware standard used by ROS2 for communication.

**Domain Randomization**: Technique of varying simulation parameters to improve sim-to-real transfer.

---

## E

**Embodied Intelligence**: Intelligence that emerges from the interaction between an agent's body and its environment.

**End-Effector**: The device at the end of a robotic arm, such as a gripper or hand.

---

## F

**Forward Kinematics**: Computing end-effector position from joint angles.

**Functional Safety**: The ability of safety-related systems to perform their safety function.

---

## G

**Gait**: A pattern of leg movements used for locomotion.

**Grasp Quality**: A measure of how well a grasp can resist external forces and torques.

---

## H

**Homogeneous Transformation**: A 4x4 matrix representing rotation and translation.

**HRI (Human-Robot Interaction)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.

---

## I

**Inverse Kinematics**: Computing joint angles to achieve a desired end-effector position.

**Isaac Sim**: NVIDIA's GPU-accelerated robotics simulation platform.

---

## J

**Jacobian**: A matrix relating joint velocities to end-effector velocities.

---

## K

**Kinematics**: The study of motion without considering forces.

---

## L

**Lifecycle Node**: A ROS2 node with managed state transitions.

---

## M

**Morphological Computation**: Information processing performed by the body's physical structure.

---

## N

**Null Space**: The space of joint motions that do not affect end-effector position.

---

## O

**Operational Space**: The task-space representation of robot motion.

---

## P

**PhysX**: NVIDIA's physics simulation engine used in Isaac Sim and Unity.

**Proprioception**: Sensing the position and motion of one's own body.

---

## Q

**QoS (Quality of Service)**: ROS2 communication policies for reliability and latency.

**Quaternion**: A four-parameter representation of rotation avoiding gimbal lock.

---

## R

**ROS2**: Robot Operating System 2, a middleware framework for robot software.

**Redundancy**: Having more degrees of freedom than required for a task.

---

## S

**SDF (Simulation Description Format)**: XML format for describing robot models in Gazebo.

**Sim-to-Real**: Transferring behaviors learned in simulation to real robots.

**SIL (Safety Integrity Level)**: A measure of safety system reliability.

---

## T

**Task-Priority Control**: Hierarchical control where higher-priority tasks take precedence.

**Torque Control**: Controlling a robot by commanding joint torques directly.

---

## U

**URDF (Unified Robot Description Format)**: XML format for describing robot models.

---

## V

**VLA (Vision-Language-Action)**: Models that process visual and language inputs to produce robot actions.

**Visual Servoing**: Using visual feedback to control robot motion.

---

## W

**Whole-Body Control**: Coordinated control of all robot joints for complex tasks.

---

## Z

**ZMP (Zero Moment Point)**: A stability criterion for bipedal walking.

---

*This glossary is under development. Additional terms will be added as chapters are completed.*
